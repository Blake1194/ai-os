# Daily Operating Loop Commands

## Purpose
These commands support the first practical Atlas daily operating loop. They are designed to be plain English, reusable, and easy to copy/paste.

## Startup
- "Run Atlas startup: review active tasks, recent logs, blockers, and the next best action."
- "Start my Atlas day and identify the top one to three goals from current context."

## Task Intake
- "Intake this request into Atlas, classify it, and tell me whether it should become a task, note, decision, workflow, or command."
- "Turn this new request into a live Atlas task instance and route it through the system."

## Execution Handoff
- "Use Atlas command routing on this task, choose the right layer, and execute all safe reversible steps."
- "Move this task from planning into execution and stop before any irreversible action."

## Approval Request
- "Atlas has reached the approval boundary. Show me what is prepared and what exact final action is waiting."
- "Pause here and tell me the irreversible step you did not take."

## End-of-Day Review
- "Run Atlas end-of-day review: summarize what was attempted, completed, changed, blocked, and what rolls into tomorrow."
- "Create today’s session review and identify the next best action for the next session."

## Weekly Cleanup Review
- "Run Atlas weekly stabilization: review duplication, stale notes, reusable patterns, and workflow candidates."
- "Identify what should be cleaned up, promoted into commands, or converted into workflows this week."
